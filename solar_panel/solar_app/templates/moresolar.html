Sunlight to Power

Photovoltaic Cells

Photovoltaic cells are semiconductor devices
that directly convert sunlight into electricity
through the photovoltaic effect. These cells
are typically made of crystalline silicon,
though other materials like thin-film technology
may also be employed.

Sunlight Sparks Electric Current in PV Cells

When sunlight, which is composed of photons,
strikes the surface of the PV cells, it excites
the electrons within the semiconductor material.
This energy absorption causes the electrons to move,
creating an electric current.

Powerful Solar Cells

Solar Cells Amplifying Power

As the excited electrons move within the
semiconductor material, they generate a
flow of direct current (DC) electricity.
The DC electricity produced by individual cells
is relatively low, so multiple cells are
interconnected within a solar panel to enhance
the overall power output.
