Smart Energy Release

Controlled Energy Release

When the need for illumination arises, the
stored energy in the battery is carefully
released. This process is controlled by
an inverter, which converts the DC electricity
stored in the battery into alternating
current (AC), the standard form of electricity
used in homes and businesses.

Sunlit Glow: Solar-Powered Bulb

The AC electricity is then supplied to a light
bulb. As the current flows through the bulb's
filament or other lighting technology,
it produces visible light. The bulb lights up,
providing a reliable and efficient source
of illumination.

Discharge for Power Needs

Sun to Battery Power: Lighting Up the Night

When energy demand exceeds the current solar
production, or during nighttime when there
is no sunlight, the stored energy in the
batteries is discharged. The inverter converts
the stored DC energy back into AC, making
it suitable for powering appliances and devices.
